Pay as You Go

Nov 5th, 2011 | Comments

So by the end of 2011 I will have spent roughly 1/2 my time the year outside of the United States.  Paying monthly for Verizon is seeming unnecessary for me.  I don’t talk on the phone very much and I’ve gotten used to slow Internet access on my phone often when I’m abroad. I began investigating ways to save money on my phone and researched pay as you go plans.  My requirements were that I had to be able to buy a SIM Card (no CDMA networks) and it had to be cheaper than what I was paying monthly.

So finally I decided on T-Mobile and ordered a SIM card.  I paid for overnight shipping so the total was $19.40.  Not too bad, though if I had gone to a store I could have saved the $11.00 in shipping costs, but T-Mobile said they couldn’t process my order.  Why they have to background check me for a prepaid card I don’t know.  I suppose to make sure I’m not a drug-dealer or something.  So I ended up going to a store and purchasing a SIM card for 6.99 + Tax and a $50.00 credit.

So next up porting my old number to Google Voice.  I decided this was the easiest thing to do because then I wouldn’t have to notify anyone of a new number AND when I’m abroad people can contact me like they would at home.  The first step was to get into my Verizon account and find out my account number.  I had not reset my password to Verizon’s website in about a year and just paid the bill from my phone. When I finally looked into my Mobile expenses it turns out I was paying $161.57 a month! Though I did have a Mifi and a phone (yes, the above revelation shows something about my personal bookkeeping).  Anyway then I went through the process with Google Voice to port my mobile number.  When it says you may have a disruption in receiving calls and texts for up to three days they mean it.  Much hilarity ensued when I texted people and never received their response.  This was combined with the fact that I didn’t have a phone that would work with my new SIM card at the time.  Anyway, none of the mishaps were the end of the world and it was a good reminder of how dependent we can be on technology.

So now that I’m all set-up to pay as I go, I’m off on another trip abroad.
